Troop 570 of Kirkland is hosting a one-night car camping trip for Webelos interested in our troop

The Invitation

The Boy Troop 570 and the Girl Troop 570 of Kirkland are hosting a one-night car camping trip at Fort Flagler Historical State Park on Marrowstone Island on October 9 and 10.

All Webelos and Arrow of Light Scouts are invited to come along with a parent and join us for one of our more exciting trips of the year!

Activities

We have many activities planned for the weekend. The Scouts will teach the Webelos skills like

  • fire building
  • knife & axe safety
  • knots & lashings
  • orienteering and more

On Saturday evening, Troop 570 will cook dinner for you. Afterwards, there is going to be an evening campfire program with skits, songs and stories. Webelos are encouraged to participate by adding their own skits, songs or run-ons.

Gear

Bring your Webelos handbook as you will have the chance to work on some activity badge requirements.

Prepare as you would for outdoor overnight Fall outing. The standard BSA Webelos Scout Overnight Checklist in the Cub Scout Leaders Book (page 34-50) is a good one to use. You can also download the checklist from scouting.org.

Bring a tent if you have one or let us know if you want to borrow a tent when you RSVP.

Coordinate whatever group equipment you might need including but not limited to: Tents with poles, stakes, ground cloths and lines, dining fly (in case of liquid sunshine), cooking/cleaning kits, stoves. If you feel you are lacking any other gear, please contact us as our Troop probably has it and would be happy to let you borrow it for the weekend.

You are encouraged to put together a skit, song or run-on for the Saturday evening campfire program. Make sure you bring any props you might need for it.

Transportation Details

We will meet at 8:30 am on Saturday morning, October 9th at the north end of the` Kingsgate Park and Ride parking lot. We will be returning around 2:00 pm on Sunday afternoon, October 10th.

If you need help with driving, let us know. We will assume your group is handling your own transportation unless you tell us otherwise. Troop 570 can help you transport your gear from the Camp Pigott parking lot to the camping area.

Covid-19 Safety

Troop 570 will observe pandemic safety protocols as determined by the Boy Scouts of America national organization as well as the Chief Seattle Council. All participants must be either vaccinated or had a covid test 72 hours before the event. Masks may be required and scouts are always welcome to wear a mask if they feel it is appropriate for them.

Questions

If you have any questions and/or plan on attending, please contact either your Den Leader or the Troop 570 Webelos Liaison at t570_webelos@outlook.com.

Fee

The fee for the campout is $5 per person.

We hope to see you there!

All Webelos are invited to come along and join us with a parent for one of our more exciting trips of the year. We have many activities planned for the weekend. We will be teaching and practicing scout skills that might include fire building, knife and axe safety, knot tying, cooking, pioneering and orienteering – plus maybe a few surprises. We will make time for some group games too.

We hope you will join us!

Travel

Meet us at the north end of the Kingsgate Park & Ride at 9:30 AM on Saturday, November 2nd. Plan on leaving the parking lot promptly by 10:00 AM when all have arrived.

The drive to Camp Pigott is approximately 45 minutes from Kirkland (map). If you need help with driving, let us know. We will assume your group is handling your own transportation unless you tell us otherwise.

Troop 570 will help you transport your gear from the Camp Pigott parking lot to the camping area.

We plan to return to the Kingsgate Park & Ride at (or around) 1:00 pm on Sunday, November 3rd.

Meals

Bring your own "sack" lunch for Saturday and any snacks that you might want.

Troop 570 will cook Saturday dinner and Sunday breakfast! Adults are encouraged to visit with us as we prepare the evening meal. We charge $5 per person to cover the cost of the meals.

Everyone should have extra food for themselves in case of emergency (extra food is one of the 10 essentials).
